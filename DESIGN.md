# Online Everywhere — Brand Design System

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

**Strategic Authority meets Caribbean warmth.** The visual identity projects institutional credibility (deep navy, clean grids) while feeling approachable and local. Think: a Bajan business owner's trusted advisor — not a cold Silicon Valley SaaS.

- **Authoritative**: Bold headlines, structured layouts, confident spacing
- **Luminous**: Glassmorphism surfaces, subtle gradients, radial highlights
- **Local**: Warm accents, island-appropriate metaphors (crop over, sea, sun)
- **Modern**: Clean sans-serif, generous whitespace, high contrast

## 2. Tone & Voice

| Moment | Tone | How it reads |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Educational | Authoritative but warm | "Here's what the data says" — expert sharing, not lecturing |
| Urgent / Crisis | Direct, concerned | "78.7% of Bajan businesses are one algorithm change away from invisibility" |
| Celebratory | Genuine, proud | Local wins, client success, Barbados achievements |
| Lead magnet | Confident, specific | "Download the free audit. 15 minutes. No obligation." |
| Engagement | Curious, inviting | "We asked 50 Bajan business owners. The answer surprised us." |
| Trend commentary | Timely, sharp | Ties local events to broader digital shifts |
| Seasonal / Event | Context-aware | Crop Over = "Your brand deserves a costume too" |

**Core rules:**
- Never corporate or robotic
- Never jargon-heavy
- Always reference Barbados context when relevant
- Punchy sentences. One idea per paragraph.
- Quantify everything (78.7%, 0.4s, 4.8x ROAS, $0/mo)

## 3. Color Roles

| Token | Role | Usage |
|-------|------|-------|
| `google-blue` (#4285F4) | Primary action | CTAs, links, active states, data highlights |
| `google-green` (#34A853) | Success | Positive metrics, growth indicators, checkmarks |
| `google-red` (#EA4335) | Alert | Warnings, urgency markers, crisis stats |
| `google-yellow` (#FBBC05) | Accent | Highlights, attention-grabbers, special offers |
| `navy-deep` (#202124) | Primary text | Headlines, body copy on light backgrounds |
| `navy-muted` (#5F6368) | Secondary text | Supporting copy, metadata, captions |
| `surface` (#f7f9ff) | Background | Page/slide backgrounds |
| `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) | Card surface | Content cards, highlight boxes |

**Do:** Use google-blue for the main CTA on every asset
**Do:** Use google-red sparingly — only for urgency/statistics that demand attention
**Do:** Keep backgrounds light and clean (surface tones)
**Don't:** Mix more than 3 brand colors in one asset
**Don't:** Use navy-deep as a background color (reserve for text)

## 4. Layout Principles

- **Rule of thirds** for text placement — left or center aligned, never right
- **Safe zones**: 10% padding on all sides — never place text or key visual elements outside these margins
- **Visual hierarchy**: Headline → Stat/Number → Body → CTA (top to bottom)
- **White space**: Generous. One concept per section. Don't crowd.
- **Image-first**: When using photography, let it breathe — overlay text only in designated zones
- **CTA placement**: Bottom third of the asset, clearly separated, using google-blue button or prominent link

**For social media graphics specifically:**
- 1200×1200px for square posts
- 1080×1080px for carousel slides
- Text overlay area on left/center with space for headline
- Bottom 20% reserved for CTA + logo
- Do NOT make it look like a website UI or app interface — it's a social media graphic

## 5. Component Stylings

### Cards / Content Blocks
- Background: white (`#ffffff`)
- Radius: `rounded-lg` (16px)
- Shadow: soft (`0 8px 32px 0 rgba(31, 38, 135, 0.07)`)
- Border: 1px solid `outline-variant`

### Buttons / CTAs
- Primary: `google-blue` fill, white text, `rounded-md` (8px)
- Hover: subtle scale (1.02), intensify shadow
- Text: `label-bold` (Inter, 14px, 700, 0.05em letter-spacing)

### Stat/Number Highlights
- Font: `display-lg` (Plus Jakarta Sans, 48px, 800)
- Color: `google-blue` for positive, `google-red` for urgent
- Context label below: `label-sm` in `navy-muted`

### Data Visualizations
- Bar charts: `google-blue` primary bars, `google-green` for positive trends
- Line graphs: Clean, thin strokes, `google-blue` line
- Pie/Donut: Use brand palette only (max 4 slices)

## 6. Depth & Elevation

- **Flat is preferred** — minimal shadows for social media
- **Glass effect**: White fill + soft shadow + subtle border for featured content blocks
- **Radial gradients**: Use at top of assets to simulate a light source
- **No aggressive drop shadows** — keep it clean

## 7. Typography

- **Headlines**: Plus Jakarta Sans, 800 weight, tight letter-spacing
- **Body**: Inter, 400 weight, 16px, 1.6 line-height
- **Stats/Numbers**: Plus Jakarta Sans, 800 weight, 48px+
- **Labels**: Inter, 700 weight, 14px, uppercase 0.05em tracking

## 8. Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Lead every asset with a quantified hook or bold statement
- Use Barbados context whenever possible (references to local culture, business climate, geography)
- Keep it clean — one message per asset
- Use generous whitespace
- End with a clear, single CTA

**Don't:**
- Make it look like a generic Canva template
- Use stock photography that looks obviously stock
- Crowd the design — if it feels busy, remove elements
- Use dark backgrounds for text-heavy assets
- Mix multiple fonts beyond the brand pair (Plus Jakarta Sans + Inter)

## 9. Responsive & Platform Adaptation

- **LinkedIn**: 1200×1200 square preferred
- **Instagram**: 1080×1080 square or 1080×1350 portrait
- **Carousel**: Each slide should work standalone but tell a narrative as a set
- **Text scaling**: Headlines must be readable at 30% of full size (thumbnail view)

## 10. Brand Voice Samples

Reference these as quality benchmarks for tone and structure:

**Hook examples:**
- "78.7% of Bajan businesses are one Instagram update away from disappearing."
- "Your website loads in 4.2 seconds. Your competitors load in 0.4. Want to guess who gets the sale?"
- "The Barbados government will pay 75% of your digital transformation. Here's the catch."
- "We asked 50 small business owners in Barbados: 'What's your biggest marketing headache?'"

**CTA examples:**
- "Book your free digital audit → onlineverywhere.com"
- "Download the 2024 Barbados Digital Benchmark Report"
- "15-minute assessment. No jargon. No obligation."
